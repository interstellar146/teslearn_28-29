1. Projectile Motion:

Projectile motion refers to the motion of an object that is projected into the air and moves under the influence of gravity alone. The path followed by the object is called its trajectory, which is typically a parabola.

Examples include:

A ball thrown into the air
A cricket shot
A cannonball fired at an angle

Projectile motion is a combination of two independent motions:

➤ Horizontal Motion:
No acceleration
Constant velocity
➤ Vertical Motion:
Uniform acceleration due to gravity
Velocity changes continuously

👉 These two motions occur simultaneously but independently
